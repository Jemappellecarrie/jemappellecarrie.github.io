I am an M.S. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Duke University. Before coming to Duke, I received my B.S. in Games from the University of Utah.

I am fortunate to work with Prof. Ehsan Abadi and Prof. Navid NaderiAlizadeh at Duke University on reinforcement learning for CT protocol optimization. I also collaborate with Prof. Huaizu Jiang at Northeastern University on human–object interaction in soccer. Previously, I worked with Prof. Weitong Zhang at UNC–Chapel Hill on transformer-based trajectory modeling and data augmentation for offline reinforcement learning.

Research interests. Broadly, my research lies at the intersection of reinforcement learning and computer vision. I am particularly interested in learning from visual and sequential data, developing reliable decision-making methods, and translating them to real-world applications, for instance, medical imaging and human–object interaction.
