I am an M.S. student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Duke University. Before coming to Duke, I received my B.S. in Games from the University of Utah.

I am fortunate to work with Prof. Ehsan Abadi and Prof. Navid NaderiAlizadeh at Duke University on reinforcement learning for CT protocol optimization. I also collaborate with Prof. Huaizu Jiang at Northeastern University on human–object interaction in soccer. Previously, I worked with Prof. Weitong Zhang at UNC–Chapel Hill on transformer-based trajectory modeling and data augmentation for offline reinforcement learning.

Research interests. My research focuses on building intelligent systems that can learn useful representations and make reliable decisions from visual and sequential data. I am broadly interested in reinforcement learning, representation learning, and diffusion models, particularly in how generative modeling can support perception and policy learning under limited or imperfect data. A central direction of my work is human–object interaction, where I study how models understand people, objects, and their interactions over time, with broader applications to medical imaging and embodied intelligence.
